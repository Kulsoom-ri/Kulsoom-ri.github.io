Posture Correcting Chair

Chairs are the most common furniture on which people sit, work and take rest. Poor posture can lead to postural instability (e.g., lack of balance) as a person ages. Posture Correcting Chair was a simple innovation developed in 2013, which by the use of bio-sensors monitored a person's posture and further alerted the user if he/she was sitting in the wrong posture. This innovation was acknowledged on a national level by the National Innovation Foundation (NIF).
AWARDS : 'IGNITE 2013' Award received at the hands of former President of India, late Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam.
'9th Biennial Award for Grassroots Innovation' received at the hands of former President of India, Dr. Pranab Mukherjee.

Detection of Dysgraphia

Dysgraphia is a learning disorder viewed largely as a handwriting impairment. Unfortunately, symptoms of dysgraphia are often overlooked by educators, and students with the condition are viewed as simply being unmotivated or messy. Several tests exist to diagnose dysgraphia however, the major drawbacks of these tests are subjective examination, time-consuming and use of standardized text.
'Detection of Dysgraphia' was a project aimed at combating these drawbacks. The project made use of Cloud AutoML Vision Object Detection technology and an artificial intellgience model was developed aimed at identifying 6 traits of Dysgraphia. The project is still under development with features such as additional indentification traits as well as detection in different languages being explored.
AWARDS : Winner of 'Google Code to Learn 2019'.

ALIVE?

Related by means of poetry, music and interviews, 'Alive?' is the story of Assamese migrants living in the Hindi speaking belt of India. Exploited financially by the locals, the people live in extreme conditions of social exclusion and poverty. The theme, 'I am like the dust' is a comparison between the migrants and dust, which is blown away from everywhere and has no home of its own. The people question the meaning of their lives repeatedly while their misery goes on, unnoticed and unseen by the world. The film ends questioning whether such a life is a life at all.
AWARDS : The film was chosen as a finalist in the 'Dreamers category' in Duemila30 Youth Film Festival for a Sustainable Revolution in collaboration with the United Nations Information Centre. In addition to the screenings of films, the festival also consisted of workshops with Oscar-winning directors, panel discussion on 2030 Sustainable Development Goals with UNRIC- Italia and Casa Emergency (Italian Humanitarian Association) as well as the opportunity to work with social incubators like FabriQ.

Who is a refugee?

The language we use to talk about refugees matters.
Today, the language of invasion has become increasingly mainstream with headlines around the world proclaiming that nations are being taken over by 'swarms' and 'floods' of refugees. This type of language stigmatizes those crossing borders and legitimizes hatred, racism and xenophobia towards them.
The short film 'who is a refugee?' brings this issue to the forefront by questioning the terms that have been used in association with the refugee crisis, some with xenophobic notions and attempts to make the point that in the end they are simply humans.
AWARDS : Winner of 'International Jury Award' at PLURAL+ Youth Video Festival 2020 organized by the United Nations Alliance of Civilizations (UNAOC) and the International Organization for Migration (IOM).
